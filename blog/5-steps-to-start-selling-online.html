5 steps to start selling Online

Michael Matambo
|
August 24, 2020

Starting an online store might be overwhelming, so we've broken down the 10 steps needed to start an online store. 

We are going to touch on branding, web design, and content strategy. These steps will help you reach your business goals.

‍

1. Find or create a product

The first step to your e-commerce journey is to figure out what you're going to sell.

We encourage you to find something that you are passionate about, something that excites you, something that you enjoy. Once you find the product or service you want to sell, make sure to stick to that niche.

Whatever your product or niche, do extensive research about the products you're selling and the market you are selling in.  Knowing your market will make a difference between succeeding or failing. 

Find a niche that is not oversaturated, as you will struggle to compete with players with a huge marketing budget.

2. Know your audience

Understanding your audience is the key to a successful online store. Know details about your potential client like their age, income range, and gender would help you target those ads.

User experience comes hand in hand with knowing your audience as the website design; social media content, logo, branding have to be tailored to your potential customers.

‍

3. Come up with a good name

Be original and come up with a good name. One that is easy to remember, easy to spell, and is related to your product. Once you have your name, you can go ahead and register the domain name. You can purchase your domain name with godaddy, google domain. We suggest that you purchase your domain name instead of doing it later, that URL might be snatched and you might have to restart the process of looking for the right name.

‍

4. Branding

Branding is your business personality; things like logos, flyers, and websites will help you have a tighter connection with your customers. You build brand loyalty within your audience. 

An e-commerce website needs to be distinct and memorable, and you can do this using a colour palette and a good web design. Building a strong brand is vital as you have to gain your client's trust. Most of us are hesitant to enter our credit card number into websites you have never heard of.


5.Find the right eCommerce platform

With various ecommerce platforms, like Shopify, WordPress and Webflow,  choosing an eCommerce platform even more difficult. We are going to briefly outline the benefits and disadvantages between the different ecommerce platform.

Shopify is a great platform to get started with as you choose the theme you want, drag and drop type interface is used to edited the theme. The only disadvantage is that the themes are limited, as 95% of Shopify stores look alike. 

WordPress is a good platform. You can host a WordPress website for free and find 1000’s of themes that you can choose. One significant disadvantage is maintaining the site is a hassle. One update can easily break the site.

Webflow is an excellent platform. Webflow has the same price as Shopify, but you get much more freedom to design whatever your heart desires. Webflow allows you to create a custom website Website that is fast and mobile responsive. The only disadvantage is that you will have to hire a web designer. You can still use themes with Webflow, but the learning curve is steep.
